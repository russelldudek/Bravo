90-day entry plan

Earn trust before you standardize.

A practical entry plan for a Bravo Director joining a portfolio of growth-stage brands. The plan assumes the exact client mix and SOWs will vary; milestones are designed around engagement leadership rather than a single operating context.

0-30

Read & earn trust

Outcome: a shared picture of the system and the engagement.

  • Review SOW, objectives, budgeted hours, current deliverables, and Bravo team roles.
  • Meet founder/CEO, finance, sales, operations, and key external partners.
  • Shadow current operating cadences and tactical work.
  • Map demand, production, procurement, inventory, fulfillment, cash, partners, systems, data, and decision rights.
  • Define the top service, cash, margin, quality, and partner risks.
  • Establish client communication norms and escalation expectations.
  • Build a 30-day learning agenda and validate assumptions visibly.
31-60

Stabilize & instrument

Outcome: fewer surprises and faster decisions around the real constraints.

  • Stand up an exception cadence for shortages, late production, inventory discrepancies, OTIF risk, and cash-critical buys.
  • Create one-owner/one-next-action discipline for critical constraints.
  • Repair the few data definitions that block useful decisions.
  • Implement partner issue aging and escalation paths.
  • Build the initial operating scorecard and decision calendar.
  • Clarify delegation to junior Bravo team members and coach on expected outcomes, not task volume alone.
  • Review client hours against SOW value and risk.
61-90

Build & transfer

Outcome: the client and Bravo team can run the mechanism without constant senior intervention.

  • Codify the minimum viable forecasting/S&OP, inventory, partner, cost, and systems routines appropriate to the client stage.
  • Document decision rights, escalation thresholds, and standard work in the client's actual tools.
  • Train and observe the people inheriting daily execution.
  • Review outcome evidence against SOW, budget, and client expectations.
  • Close gaps in ownership and partner accountability.
  • Agree next-state roadmap: maintain, deepen, re-scope, or hand off.
  • Capture reusable lessons for Bravo without exposing client-confidential information.
Cross-client operating discipline

Protect senior attention from noise.

Across 2-4 clients, I would maintain a bounded portfolio view of consequence, cash exposure, decision latency, SOW commitment, and team load. The purpose is not to create a universal score. It is to make the weekly allocation of senior attention explicit and discussable.
