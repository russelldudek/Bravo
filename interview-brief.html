Interview thesis brief

Embedded ownership, without permanent dependence.

A compact briefing on the mandate, company moment, operating tensions, evidence transfer, candidate thesis, 90-day entry plan, and hard objection.

Mandate summary

Run several businesses at once - without treating any of them generically.

The Director is effectively the senior operations leader and client account owner across multiple growth-stage brands. The job combines strategic planning, S&OP, partner management, cost and margin work, systems/process design, junior-team leverage, client trust, budget discipline, and tactical execution when needed.

Company moment

Bravo is scaling a fractional operating model built on embedded teams and accumulated pattern recognition.

Public materials position Bravo as a one-stop fractional operations partner for growth-stage consumer brands, with hands-on ownership, senior guidance, right-sized teams, playbooks from 225+ brand engagements, and elastic capacity. That creates a strategic burden: keep the service genuinely embedded and bespoke while converting experience into leverage across many concurrent clients.

Customization vs. repeatability

Use playbooks to accelerate learning, not to force identical answers.

Strategy vs. tactics

Stay at the level of economic and operating decisions while remaining credible in the work.

Trust vs. accountability

Protect founder relationships while making decision delay and weak ownership visible.

Portfolio breadth vs. depth

Allocate senior attention by consequence and SOW, not by noise.

White-space thesis
Pattern recognition without pattern imposition.

A Bravo Director should be able to recognize familiar failure patterns quickly, but still earn the right to prescribe by understanding the client's stage, channel mix, economics, partner network, decision rights, team capability, and data reality.

Operating model

Client Ops Runbook

Read the System → Stabilize the Flow → Build the Mechanism → Transfer the Capability. The model is meant to frame engagement leadership, not replace Bravo's existing playbooks.

Read

Map flow, decisions, risk, data, partners, ownership, and SOW outcomes.

Stabilize

Protect service and cash through exception cadence, constraint ownership, and escalation.

Build

Install stage-appropriate forecasting, S&OP, cost, inventory, partner, and systems mechanisms.

Transfer

Document, coach, hand off, and verify that the system works without heroic dependency.

Proof map

Four evidence threads

The case is based on verified adjacent evidence, not invented CPG claims.

Vape-Jet

Current growth-stage operations

Production, purchasing, quality, support, customer success, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, workflow tools, telemetry, dashboards, training, and accountability.

Amazon

High-velocity customer-backward execution

24/7 operating environment, approximately five million second-day orders annually, labor planning, throughput visibility, escalation, standard work, Lean, and Gemba.

Compunetics

Manufacturing, quality, and technical program consequence

Engineering, manufacturing, quality systems, customer delivery, root cause, process control, and high-consequence stakeholders.

Cardinal Building Products

Distributed operations and fulfillment

Built a remote region and modernized commercial, CRM, fulfillment, logistics, and customer workflows.

Hard objection

No traditional CPG career

Answer: acknowledge it. Make the transferability argument through growth-stage operations, production, purchasing, quality, logistics, fulfillment, partner coordination, systems integration, data-driven mechanisms, and team development. Identify the ramp owed: retailer mechanics, routing/chargeback detail, category-specific forecasting, co-man economics, shelf-life/MOQ patterns, and Bravo's own playbooks.

90-day plan

Trust → Control → Capability

0-30: learn the client system and confirm the SOW outcomes. 31-60: stabilize constraints and instrument the decisions. 61-90: codify the right mechanisms, coach owners, and agree the next-state roadmap.
