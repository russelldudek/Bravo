Hard objection analysis

Do not hide the gap. Bound it.

The strongest plausible hiring concern is legitimate: Bravo asks for deep CPG experience, and Russell's verified career evidence is adjacent rather than traditional CPG leadership. The campaign should make the transferability case precisely and let the hiring team judge it.

Objection 01

“He has not spent 10+ years inside CPG.”

Correct. Never rewrite adjacent operations experience as direct CPG experience.

Transferability evidence: current growth-stage Director of Operations scope across production, purchasing, quality, customer success, support, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, workflow systems, telemetry, and training; Amazon 24/7 high-velocity operations; Compunetics manufacturing, quality, and customer delivery leadership; Cardinal distributed fulfillment and logistics.

Ramp owed: retailer routing and chargeback mechanics, category-specific demand patterns, shelf-life and MOQ dynamics, co-man economics, retailer/3PL vocabulary, and Bravo's own playbooks.

Interview proof strategy: demonstrate how Russell diagnoses an unfamiliar system, separates facts from assumptions, establishes decision cadence, and uses experts without outsourcing judgment.

Objection 02 · Multi-client consulting context

Concern: strong operator, but can he manage several clients and commercial relationships simultaneously?

Response: show explicit portfolio prioritization, SOW discipline, delegation, remote operating cadence, and communication architecture. Use the Engagement Portfolio Simulator as a discussion artifact, labeled as a hypothesis rather than Bravo process.

Objection 03 · Demand planning depth

Concern: the role asks for 3+ years of demand planning and production management experience.

Response: do not invent a forecasting tenure claim. Anchor on direct production management, labor/throughput planning, supply/logistics work, data-to-decision systems, and discuss the specific forecasting methods and CPG planning cadence Russell would need to ramp into under Bravo's playbooks.

Objection 04 · 1099 part-time fit

Concern: can a senior operations executive operate effectively within a minimum 15-hour/week contract model?

Response: frame the value as senior judgment, deliberate attention allocation, mechanism-building, and team leverage - not a claim that every client problem can be solved by personal task volume.

Objection 05 · AI distraction risk

Concern: will an AI-forward operator overcomplicate practical CPG work?

Response: keep AI subordinate to the operating problem. Use it only where it can reduce analyst drag or improve exception handling, with human authority retained over commitments, overrides, escalations, and client communication.
