Own the outcome, not merely the recommendation.

Bravo is not hiring a conventional consultant. It is hiring a senior embedded operator who can earn founder trust, direct junior capacity, manage engagement economics, and still step into the tactical work when consequence demands it.

The operating tensions

The job lives between competing truths.

Bespoke client realityReusable Bravo playbooks

Recognize patterns quickly without forcing yesterday’s answer onto a different stage, category, channel mix, or partner network.

Strategic leadershipTactical credibility

Discuss cash, roadmap, and risk with founders—then audit the invoice, review inventory, or unblock freight when needed.

Founder empathyOperational accountability

Respect the pressure while making missed commitments, decision latency, and weak ownership visible enough to change.

Multi-client breadthConsequential depth

Allocate senior attention by consequence, reversibility, SOW commitment, and team leverage—not by whoever pings loudest.

The hard objection

“You do not come from a traditional CPG career.”

True. I would not ask Bravo to ignore that gap, and I would not manufacture brand experience I do not have.

My case is transferability under consequence: current growth-stage operations leadership; high-velocity Amazon execution; long-cycle manufacturing and quality leadership; distributed logistics and fulfillment; and a record of making complex work visible enough to improve.

The ramp I owe Bravo is specific: retailer mechanics, category forecasting, co-man economics, routing guides and chargebacks, shelf-life and MOQ dynamics, and Bravo’s own playbooks.
